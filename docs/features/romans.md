# Romans Tribe

## Overview

The **Romans** are a disciplined and advanced civilization, known for their **social organization**, **technological achievements**, and **elite troops**. They are the **easiest tribe for newcomers**, offering a **balanced mix** of offensive and defensive potential.

Roman troops are well-rounded — their **infantry is among the strongest** in the game, while their **cavalry is average** but reliable. This versatility comes at a cost: **training times are long** and **unit upkeep is expensive**, making planning and efficiency essential.

> The Romans are ideal for **new players** who prefer balanced gameplay and want to learn both offense and defense effectively.

## Playstyle Tips

* Take advantage of the **simultaneous construction** feature — you can upgrade a **building and a resource field at the same time**.
* Focus on **infantry strength** for both offense and defense early in the game.
* Invest in the **Horse Drinking Trough** to reduce cavalry crop consumption and maintain a strong army efficiently.
* Balance your economy — long training times and high costs mean you'll need **steady resource income**.
* Avoid overextending early; Romans reward **patience and precision**.

## Buildings

### City Wall (Special Building)
- **Purpose**: Provides the highest defensive bonus in the game
- **Bonus**: Significantly increases village defense against all attack types
- **Strategic Value**: Essential for defensive players and late-game protection

### Horse Drinking Trough (Special Building)
- **Purpose**: Reduces crop consumption for cavalry units
- **Bonus**: Lowers food requirements for all cavalry units
- **Strategic Value**: Allows maintaining larger cavalry armies with less food strain

## Strategy Summary

**Strengths:**
- Strong infantry for both offense and defense
- Best defensive wall in the game
- Balanced army composition
- Great for beginners learning the game
- Simultaneous construction for faster development

**Weaknesses:**
- High costs and long training times
- Lower cavalry defense against mounted units
- Requires careful resource management
- Expensive unit upkeep

> If you enjoy **well-rounded gameplay** with **elite troops and precise management**, the Romans are your perfect starting point.

## Recommended Tactics

1. **Early Game**: Focus on Legionnaire production and building your City Wall
2. **Mid Game**: Develop Praetorian defenses while training Imperians for offense
3. **Late Game**: Use Senator units for conquest and loyalty reduction
4. **Economy**: Take advantage of simultaneous construction for faster growth
5. **Defense**: Leverage City Wall bonuses to protect your empire

## Counter Strategies (For Other Tribes)

- **Cavalry Focus**: Target Roman infantry with strong cavalry units
- **Speed**: Use fast units to bypass Roman defenses before they can respond
- **Resource Denial**: Attack Roman resource production to limit their expensive unit training
- **Timing**: Strike when Romans are in the middle of long training queues

## Historical Context

The Romans were an ancient civilization that grew from a small city-state in Italy to one of the largest empires in history. Known for their military discipline, engineering achievements, and administrative organization, they built roads, aqueducts, and fortifications that lasted for centuries. Their legions were among the most effective military forces of the ancient world, combining infantry, cavalry, and engineering units in sophisticated tactics.
